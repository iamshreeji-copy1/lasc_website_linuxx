Hydrothermal Autoclaves

The SATs Hydrothermal Autoclave, with a durable nonmagnetic 316-stainless steel shell and a research-grade PTFE or PPL inner vessel, is available in sizes from 25 mL to 2500 mL. Its robust design ensures safety under high-pressure conditions, making it ideal for material synthesis, chemical reactions, and geological studies. Trust SATs for reliable performance and advanced research support.

Key Specifications

High-Quality Construction and Shell Material

Crafted from nonmagnetic 316-stainless steel, ensuring durability and resistance to corrosion.

Inner Vessel

Made from PTFE (Teflon) or PPL, both research-grade materials known for their high chemical resistance and purity.

Robust Design and Outer Shell Thickness

Robust Design and Outer Shell Thickness Designed with a higher outer shell thickness to withstand high-pressure conditions, ensuring safety and reliability during use.

Electrode Configuration
Versatile Volume Options: Internal Volume: Available in a wide range of sizes to suit various experimental needs: 25 mL, 50 mL, 100 mL, 200 mL, 500 mL, 1000 mL, 1500 mL, 2000 mL, and 2500 mL.

XYZ Positioning Control

Offers precise control in X, Y, and Z directions, facilitating accurate alignment and measurement of microelectronic components.

Compatibility with Electrical Measurement Devices:

Seamlessly connects with LCR meters, Potentiostats, and other electrical measurement devices. This capability allows comprehensive electrical characterization, including impedance spectroscopy, electrochemical measurements, and more..

Applications

Material Synthesis

Utilized in the synthesis of nanomaterials, zeolites, and other advanced materials that require high-pressure and high-temperature conditions.

Geochemical and Geological Studies

Essential for simulating natural hydrothermal processes, aiding in the study of mineral formation and other geological phenomena.

Pharmaceutical Research

Used in the development and testing of new pharmaceuticals, especially where high-pressure and high-temperature conditions are required.

Environmental Testing

Assists in the analysis of environmental samples, helping to understand the impact of high-pressure conditions on various substances.

Chemical Reactions

Facilitates hydrothermal and solvothermal reactions, allowing for the exploration of new chemical pathways and the creation of novel compounds.
